Festival of Sacrifice

Eid ul Adha commemorates the willingness of Prophet Ibrahim (Abraham) to sacrifice his son as an act of obedience to Allah. It represents faith, devotion, and submission to the divine will.

The Sacrifice

Muslims around the world perform the ritual of Qurbani (sacrifice) to honor Ibrahim's devotion. The meat is shared among family, friends, and the less fortunate, promoting unity and charity.

Spiritual Significance

This holy day teaches us about faith, sacrifice, and charity. It reminds us to put our trust in Allah and to be generous towards those in need, fostering community bonds.

Hajj Connection

Eid ul Adha marks the culmination of the annual Hajj pilgrimage to Mecca. It's a time when Muslims worldwide unite in worship and celebration, regardless of their location.
